Why united migrations exists
From panic to design
Migration is not an exception. It’s the story of humanity.

People move. For work, for safety, for love, for curiosity, for climate. The real question is not “migration or no migration”, but chaos vs. coordination, exploitation vs. fairness, fear vs. design.

United Migrations is an independent, global organisation that builds the infrastructure, standards, and alliances needed to make human mobility humane, predictable, and genuinely beneficial for everyone involved.

We are here to fix three big failures:

  • Information failure: fear and fake numbers shape policy more than evidence and lived experience.
  • Coordination failure: cities, countries, and migrants act in silos and improvise every crisis.
  • Imagination failure: we tweak old borders instead of inventing new pathways and agreements.

Lab
Migration Prototypes Lab
We co-create pilot projects: climate relocation corridors, skills-based visas, circular migration schemes, and digital-nomad compacts. Every prototype is designed with migrants, not just about them.
Partners: cities, ministries, foundations, tech & community networks.
Data
Mobility Intelligence Observatory
A living evidence hub combining research, frontline reports, and lived experience. We track what works, what fails, and where new pressure points are forming.
Output: briefs, visual maps, early-warning signals & practical playbooks.
Network
Global Pathways Alliance
A membership network for cities, organisations, and movements that want to swap improvisation for shared standards, joint projects, and coordinated messaging.
Members: municipalities, NGOs, diaspora groups, impact-driven companies.

Common questions
Quick answers, deep work

Are you a UN body or a government agency?

No. United Migrations is independent by design. We work with institutions, but we are not bound by their election cycles, PR battles, or bureaucracy.

Are you “for” or “against” migration?

We are for reality. Migration is already happening. Our work is to make it humane, predictable, and mutually beneficial, instead of letting it be weaponised as a permanent culture war.

Where are you based?

United Migrations is built as a networked organisation with anchor hubs in key regions, rather than one HQ. Our work follows the routes of movement, not the old map of power.

How will I actually get involved?

After you sign up, you’ll receive invitations to online circles grouped by interest (cities, research, lived experience, funding). From there, you can plug into pilots, campaigns, or storytelling projects that match your energy and time.
